Essential Tips for New Gardeners:

Start Small: Begin with a small, manageable plot or a few containers to avoid becoming overwhelmed.

Location is Key: Place your garden in a spot that receives 6–8 hours of direct sun, and ensure it is easily accessible for daily care.

Invest in Soil: Use high-quality potting mix for containers or a mixture of compost and composted manure for in-ground beds to provide essential nutrients.

Water Wisely: Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep root growth, rather than shallow daily waterings. Water the soil, not the leaves, to prevent disease.

Choose Easy Plants: Start with hardy, beginner-friendly plants like herbs (mint, basil), cherry tomatoes, leafy greens (spinach, lettuce), and marigolds.

Use Mulch: Apply a 2–3 inch layer of mulch (straw, shredded leaves) to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and keep roots cool.

Identify Your Zone: Know your local hardiness zone to choose plants that will thrive in your climate.
